Heart

A heart transplant is a surgical procedure in which a damaged or failing heart is replaced with a healthy heart from a deceased donor. This procedure is typically performed on individuals with end-stage heart failure, a condition in which the heart can no longer pump blood effectively to meet the body's needs.

Lungs

A lung transplant is a surgical procedure in which one or both of a person's diseased or failing lungs are replaced with healthy lungs from a deceased donor. This procedure is typically considered for individuals with severe lung diseases or conditions that significantly impair their lung function and quality of life.

Liver

A liver transplant is a surgical procedure in which a damaged or failing liver is replaced with a healthy liver from a deceased or living donor. This procedure is typically considered for individuals with end-stage liver disease, acute liver failure, or certain liver cancers when other treatments have been ineffective or are not viable.
